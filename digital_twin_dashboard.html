XAI Research Insights INTERPRETATION
MECHANICAL LOAD
Rotational speed and torque collectively explain 37.1% of failure prediction. Their interaction (power feature) adds another 18.2%, confirming that mechanical overloading is the primary failure driver.
WEAR DEGRADATION
Tool wear (11.4%) combined with wear×torque interaction (10.3%) shows that accumulated wear under high torque significantly amplifies failure risk — supporting a non-linear degradation model.
THERMAL EFFECTS
Temperature difference (process − air) accounts for 9.9% of importance, more than raw temperatures individually. This suggests thermal gradient is a better predictor than absolute temperature for heat dissipation failures.

Anomaly Detection — Method ISOLATION FOREST
HOW IT WORKS
Isolation Forest randomly partitions data using binary trees. Anomalies, being rare and different, require fewer splits to isolate. Anomaly score = average path length across all trees.
WHY UNSUPERVISED
In real factories, labeled failure data is scarce. Isolation Forest detects never-before-seen failure patterns without requiring labeled training data — ideal for early warning systems.
RESEARCH VALUE
Combining supervised RF (failure classification) with unsupervised Isolation Forest (anomaly detection) creates a dual-layer detection system — a key contribution for IEEE paper novelty.
